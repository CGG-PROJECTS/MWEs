Project Summary

Age of Acquisition (AoA) for English Multi-Word Expressions

This dataset provides the first large-scale Age of Acquisition norms for over 80,500 English Multi-Word Expressions (MWEs), generated by a GPT-4.1-mini model fine-tuned on crowdsourced human ratings (N ≈ 47,000 ratings).

The norms are valuable for psycholinguistic research, NLP, education, and language acquisition studies.

You can perform search and sort functions here for words and age ranges, and easily import the data into R and/or python direclty using the code provided below.

Brief variable descriptions

  • Utility_Value: Estimates of the perceived usefulness or expected utility of the MWE, from Wang et al. (2026).
  • Mean_Concr_Muraki_et_al: Estimates of the concreteness of the MWE, from Muraki et al. (2023).
  • Valence_GPT_probs: Estimates of emotional valence, indicating how positive or negative the MWE is, from Martínez et al. (2025).
  • GPT_Fam_probs: Estimates of familiarity, or how well known an MWE is, from Brysbaert et al. (2025).
  • Arousal_GPT_probs: Estimates of arousal, intensity, or excitement associated with the MWE, from Martínez et al. (2025).
  • COCA_Zipf: Frequency estimates based on the Corpus of Contemporary American English, from Davies (2019–).
  • Grade Level_LWV_Dale_ORourke: Estimated grade level at which children in the United States know the MWE, based on Dale and O’Rourke’s Living Word Vocabulary classroom testing.
  • AoAtestbased_B_and_B: Test-based age-of-acquisition grade-level estimates of when children know the word or expression, derived from Living Word Vocabulary direct testing of student knowledge, from Brysbaert and Biemiller (2017).
